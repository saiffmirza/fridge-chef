## Design Context

### Users
Busy home cooks juggling weeknight meals. They open the app at the end of a long day with one hand, often near a counter, while deciding what to do with what's already in the fridge. They want to feel capable and unhurried, not optimized or surveilled.

The job-to-be-done: "Tell me what I can cook with what I already have, and don't let things rot." Speed and clarity matter more than novelty. The interface should feel like a trusted notebook, not a dashboard.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **warm, tactile, dependable.**

Voice: friendly but not chatty — short, lowercase-where-natural sentences. Uses verbs over labels ("cook something" beats "Get Recipe Suggestions"). Never marketing-speak, never patronizing.

Emotional goals: appetite, calm, a little craft-pride. The app should feel like the well-worn enamel jar on a kitchen shelf.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm organic / kitchen-y.** Tactile, hand-feel. Light-only, paper-led.

- **Palette**: warm cream paper background, ink-charcoal text, terracotta as primary accent, olive as secondary, butter-yellow as a tertiary highlight. Tinted neutrals — no pure white, no pure black.
- **Geometry**: generously rounded but not pill-bubbly (8–14px radii). Sticker-feel chips for ingredient tags. Hairline rules over heavy borders.
- **Texture**: subtle paper grain on the canvas, never on type or buttons. Soft, low-spread shadows that read as light, not as drop-shadow stickers.
- **Typography**: a distinctive humanist or warm-grotesk display paired with a refined body — handled via expo-font (Fraunces / DM Serif for display, Inter alternative like Geist or Manrope for body, or similar Google fonts that load reliably on Expo web).
- **Composition**: left-aligned, asymmetric, generous breathing room. Ingredients are the hero — the chrome recedes.

**Anti-references**: Material green (#4CAF50 and similar), generic AI gradients (purple→blue), glassmorphism, hero-metric layouts with giant numbers.

### Design Principles
1. **Paper, not glass.** Warm cream canvas, ink text, hairline divisions. No glossy cards, no glass blur, no neon glow.
2. **Earth over electric.** Terracotta + olive + butter on cream — never material green, never gradient accents.
3. **Ingredients are the hero.** Type-led lists with sticker-chip tags. Avoid boxes-inside-boxes; let the page breathe.
4. **Verbs, not labels.** "cook something", "add to fridge", "running low" — copy reads like a person, not a settings panel.
5. **Tactile, never bouncy.** Soft fades, exponential easing, gentle spring at most. No elastic overshoot.
6. **One-hand weeknight ergonomics.** Large tap targets, primary actions reachable with the thumb, expiry status legible at a glance.
